My Books

Nobody Knows

This short novelisation of Hirokazu Kore-eda's award-winning film tells the heartbreaking tale of four Tokyo siblings secretly abandoned by their mother. Forced to live alone in a small apartment, the children create their own fragile world sharing chores, rationing money and clinging to hope, while society around them remains oblivious to their existence.

Fantastic Mr Fox

Roald Dahl's 1970 children's classic follows the quick-witted Mr Fox, who nightly raids the storehouses of three cruel farmers,Boggis, Bunce and Bean,to feed his family. When the farmers unite to dig him out, Mr Fox leads the woodland animals in an underground caper that celebrates ingenuity, community and sticking it to bullies.

Oldboy

Originally a Japanese manga by Garon Tsuchiya and Nobuaki Minegishi, Oldboy centres on a man who is kidnapped, imprisoned for ten years without explanation, then suddenly released. The story tracks his obsessive quest to uncover the reason for his captivity and avenge himself, blending noir atmosphere with psychological cat and mouse twists.

White Nights

Fyodor Dostoevsky's 1848 novella is a tender, melancholic romance set during St Petersburg's luminous summer nights. A lonely “dreamer” meets Nastenka, a young woman waiting for her lover's return, and over four successive evenings pours out his soul to her. Themes of isolation, idealised love and the gulf between dreams and reality make it an early gem of Dostoevsky's work.
